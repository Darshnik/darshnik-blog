There's a moment, somewhere around your mid-twenties, when you realize that the most interesting things you learn don't come from any single source. They come from the collisions between fields — the place where cricket strategy meets game theory, where ancient trade routes explain modern geopolitics, where a database architecture decision mirrors a biological system.

I've been collecting these connections in my head for years. It's time to start writing them down.

## What to expect

This blog will cover four broad areas:

- **Sport** — primarily cricket, but also the broader patterns of competition, strategy, and what sports reveal about human psychology
- **History** — the stories and patterns that shaped the world we live in, told in a way that makes you care
- **Tech** — the ideas behind the systems we build, explained accessibly, not just for engineers
- **The future** — where things are going, what that means, and why it matters

## The format

I'm going for depth over frequency. Expect long-form essays — the kind you'd read on a weekend morning with coffee. I want each piece to teach you something or make you see something differently. If I can do both, even better.

Some posts will have diagrams. Some will have data. All of them will be written for curious humans, not specialists.

## Why now?

Honestly? I've been saying "I should start a blog" for years. I've drafted posts in Notes apps, in Google Docs, in markdown files that never saw the light of day. At some point, you just have to ship it.

So here we are. Welcome aboard.
